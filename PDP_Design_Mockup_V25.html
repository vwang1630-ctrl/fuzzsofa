The Story

Owl Chair

A quiet presence in the corner of the room.

The Owl Chair began as a sketch on a winter evening — two circles, drawn slowly, that looked more like eyes than furniture. From that simple gesture, the design unfolded: a wide, embracing seat, two rounded arms that curve inward like wings, and a back that rises just enough to rest against.

Each chair is built around a hand-welded steel frame, shaped and reshaped until the silhouette feels right. The layers that follow — high-density foam, soft down wrapping, and your choice of fabric or leather — are applied by hand, stitched and tensioned to create a seat that is as generous as it is sculptural.

It is not a chair for rushing. It asks you to sit down, stay a while, and let the world slow down around you.
